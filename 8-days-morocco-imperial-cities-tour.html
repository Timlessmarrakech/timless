8-Day Imperial Cities & Medinas: A Luxury Tour

8 Days Rabat, Fes, Marrakech Private Tour 4.9 (32 reviews)

Tour Overview

Embark on a captivating odyssey through Morocco's most venerable Imperial Cities. This meticulously curated 8-day private tour is designed by AMDK TOURS for the discerning traveler, offering an immersive cultural experience that seamlessly blends opulent comfort with authentic Moroccan heritage.

Journey through a land once ruled by powerful dynasties, from the grandeur of Rabat to the ancient, spiritual heart of Fes, and the vibrant energy of Marrakech. This thoughtfully paced expedition allows you to savor the unique essence of each Imperial Capital, guided by local experts who bring the rich history of Morocco to life.

Tour Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Casablanca

Begin your journey in cosmopolitan Casablanca, where Art Deco charm meets Islamic splendor. Morning coffee at the elegant Parc de La Ligue Arabe, an urban oasis where colonial-era design meets Moroccan flora. Explore Place Mohammed V and Place des Nations Unies, followed by shopping in the stylish Nouvelle Ville. Marvel at the Hassan II Mosque, featuring the world's tallest minaret and a glass floor overlooking the Atlantic. End your day with a gourmet dinner at the chic Corniche, enjoying fresh seafood with panoramic ocean views.

Day 2: Casablanca to Rabat

Journey to Rabat, where ancient ramparts embrace diplomatic grandeur. Wander the Andalusian Gardens of the 12th-century Oudaïa Kasbah, featuring whitewashed walls and serene gardens with sweeping views of the Bou Regreg river. Visit the Mausoleum of Mohammed V to admire its exquisite zellige tilework and carved cedar ceilings. Explore hidden souks in the medina, visit the Hassan Tower, and discover the blend of Roman ruins and Islamic architecture at the Necropolis Chellah, surrounded by lush gardens and storks.

Day 3: Rabat to Fes

Venture through Morocco's imperial heartland, starting with Meknes. Stand in awe before the monumental Bab Mansour gate, featuring intricate mosaic work. Visit the Bou Inania Medersa, then explore the remarkably preserved Roman ruins of Volubilis with its intricate mosaics and Capitoline Temple. Experience the spiritual atmosphere of Moulay Idriss, a sacred pilgrimage town, before arriving in Fes, Morocco's cultural and spiritual heart.

Day 4: Fes Exploration

Begin your day with a panoramic view of Fes from the Merenid Tombs. Enter the blue-tiled Bab Boujeloud into the ancient medina. Visit the Royal Palace of Fes with its massive brass doors, explore the 650-year-old Jewish Mellah and restored Ibn Danan Synagogue. Marvel at the 14th-century Bou Inania Medersa's stunning Marinid architecture. Witness traditional leather-making at the 11th-century Chouara Tannery, and visit Al-Qarawiyyin University, the world's oldest continually operating educational institution.

Day 5: Fes to Marrakech

Immerse yourself in Fes's culinary traditions. Begin in the aromatic spice market learning about unique Moroccan spice blends and traditional medicine. Sample freshly baked khobz from a 400-year-old communal oven, taste authentic Fassi street food including bissara and briouat, and discover an array of wild honey varieties. Experience traditional cooking methods and learn about honey's importance in Moroccan cooking and Islamic culture.

Day 6: Marrakech Discovery

Travel through the scenic Middle Atlas Mountains to Marrakech. Stop in Ifrane, known as "Little Switzerland" for its alpine architecture. Enjoy lunch in Beni Mellal featuring traditional Berber cuisine. Optional detours to cascading waterfalls and lush gorges. Arrive in Marrakech and settle into your luxury riad in the historic medina.

Day 7: Marrakech at Leisure

Explore Marrakech's iconic sites starting with the Koutoubia Mosque and its 77-meter minaret. Visit Majorelle Garden and YSL Museum with its striking blue buildings and exotic plants. Discover Berber culture at the museum housing over 600 artifacts. Tour El Bahia Palace's intricate zellige tilework and carved cedar ceilings, and the ornate 16th-century Saadian Tombs. End your day at the UNESCO-recognized Djemaa El Fna Square, experiencing its transformation from a daytime market to an evening carnival of food stalls, storytellers, and musicians.

Day 8: Departure

Reflect on your journey through Morocco's imperial cities as you transfer to Marrakech Menara Airport for your departure flight. Take with you memories of ancient medinas, architectural wonders, culinary delights, and the warm hospitality of the Moroccan people.
